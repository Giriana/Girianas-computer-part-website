Description

Wired networks use physical cables to connect devices and transmit data. They are the traditional form of networking and remain popular for their reliability and performance.

Hardware Needed

To set up a wired network, you typically need:

  • Ethernet Cables: Cat5e, Cat6, or Cat7 cables for connections
  • Network Interface Cards (NIC): Ethernet ports on devices
  • Switches/Hubs: To connect multiple devices
  • Routers: For connecting to other networks/internet
  • Patch Panels: For organized cabling in larger setups

Pros and Cons

Advantages of Wired Networks

  • Faster and more reliable connections
  • Lower latency for real-time applications
  • More secure as physical access is needed
  • Not susceptible to wireless interference
  • Generally more stable connection quality

Disadvantages of Wired Networks

  • Limited mobility due to physical cables
  • Installation can be complex, especially in large areas
  • Cables can create clutter and tripping hazards
  • More difficult to reconfigure or expand
  • Higher initial setup cost for cabling infrastructure
