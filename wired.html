Description

Wireless networks use radio waves to connect devices without physical cables. They provide mobility and flexibility, making them popular for home and business use.

Hardware Needed

To set up a wireless network, you typically need:

  • Wireless Router: The central device that broadcasts the signal
  • Access Points: For extending coverage in larger areas
  • Wireless Network Cards: Built into devices or as USB adapters
  • Repeaters/Extenders: To boost signal range if needed
  • Modem: For internet connectivity (often combined with router)

Pros and Cons

Advantages of Wireless Networks

  • Mobility - connect from anywhere within range
  • Easy to set up with minimal technical knowledge
  • No cables means cleaner installation
  • Easy to expand with additional access points
  • Supports many modern mobile devices

Disadvantages of Wireless Networks

  • Generally slower than wired connections
  • More susceptible to interference from other devices
  • Security can be more challenging to maintain
  • Signal strength affected by distance and obstacles
  • Potential health concerns about radio waves (debated)
